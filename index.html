Global change biology, Remote sensing geography, and Environmental data science.

How does global change alter the seasonal dynamics of ecosystems, and what are the cascading consequences for disturbance regimes and ecosystem functioning? These are the overarching questions driving my research. Phenology ‚Äî the timing of life cycle events ‚Äî sits at the nexus of these questions because seasonal timing governs the interactions between climate, vegetation, and ecosystem processes. It presents the most sensitive ecological responses of plants to climate warming, yet we lack advanced algorithms and tools to accurately extract phenological metrics from rapidly expanding satellite observations, and the predictive understanding of how phenological shifts interact with critical ecosystem processes like wildfire seasonality, episodic frost, and carbon cycling.

Addressing these gaps requires integrating extensive satellite and ground observations with ecological theory using advanced analytical approaches including data assimilation, deep learning, and causal inferences. My research program centers on three pillars: (1) harnessing multi-scale satellite data for detecting land surface phenology, (2) advancing mechanistic understanding of changing phenology, (3) examining how changing phenology interacts with ecosystem functioning and reshapes disturbance regimes. By linking observational data with ecological theory, my work generates both fundamental insights into ecosystem responses to global change and future projections for climate disturbance dynamics.

Research Focus

Land Surface Phenology
Multi-Source Satellite Phenology Detection
Developing algorithms that fuse geostationary (GOES), Landsat, Sentinel-2, and VIIRS observations to monitor land surface phenology at unprecedented temporal and spatial scales.
Crop Mapping
Near Real-Time Crop Progress Monitoring
Building operational systems to map corn, soybean, and other crops at field scales (~30m) in near real-time by blending crop phenometrics with growth magnitude from multi-source satellite data.
Data Fusion
Harmonized Landsat & Sentinel-2 (HLS)
Fusing optical time series from multiple sensors to create consistent, high-frequency, field-scale observations for vegetation monitoring across the CONUS and globally.
Calibration
BRDF & Reflectance Normalization
Analyzing BRDF effects on geostationary satellite reflectance (GOES ABI) and improving cross-sensor consistency through bidirectional reflectance distribution function corrections.
Ecology
Plant Phenology & Climate Interactions
Investigating spring phenology responses to late frost events, Amazon rainforest photosynthetic dynamics, and rangeland phenology for western US management.
